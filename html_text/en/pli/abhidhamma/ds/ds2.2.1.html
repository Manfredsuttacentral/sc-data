2.2.1. Introductory

Which are the states that are indeterminate?

The results of good and bad states taking effect in the universe of sense, in that of form, in that of the formless, or in [the life] which is Unincluded, and as connected with the skandhas of feeling, perception, syntheses, and intellect; as well as those inoperative states which are neither good, nor bad, nor the result of karma; all form, moreover; and [finally] Uncompounded Element—these are states that are indeterminate.
